# Kryptos Information (Wikipedia & Web Intelligence)

## Overview
Kryptos is a sculpture by American artist Jim Sanborn located on the grounds of the Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) in Langley, Virginia. Dedicated in 1990, the sculpture features a large, wave-like screen of copper inscribed with thousands of letters forming four encrypted messages (K1, K2, K3, K4). Three of these messages were solved relatively quickly by cryptanalysts, but the fourth message, K4, remains one of the most famous unsolved codes in the world.

## The Fourth Message (K4)

### Ciphertext
The K4 ciphertext consists of exactly 97 characters:
`OBKRUOXOGHULBSOLIFBBWFLRVQQPRNGKSSOTWTQSJQSSEKZZWATJKLUDIAWINFBNYPVTTMZFPKWGDKZXTJCDIGKUHUAUEKCAR`

### Official Clues Released by Jim Sanborn
Over the years, Sanborn has released specific plaintext clues to assist cryptanalysts, providing known-plaintext mapping:
- **2010**: Characters 64-69 decrypt to `BERLIN`
- **2014**: Characters 70-74 decrypt to `CLOCK` (forming `BERLINCLOCK` at positions 64-74)
- **2020**: Characters 26-34 decrypt to `NORTHEAST`
- **2020**: Characters 22-25 decrypt to `EAST` (forming `EASTNORTHEAST` at positions 22-34)

### Recent Claims and Developments (2025)
- **February 2025**: Michael P. Naughton, utilizing an AI model (Grok 3), claimed to have discovered the plaintext: `"THIS IS A GUIDE TO THE BERLIN CLOCK WHICH IS NORTHEAST OF HERE AT CIA LANGLEY, VIRGINIA"`. This string aligns with all of Sanborn's clues (`EASTNORTHEAST`, `BERLINCLOCK`).
- **November 2025**: It was reported that the original uncoded plaintext (the "solution") was discovered among Sanborn's papers and subsequently auctioned off for nearly $1 million. The buyer remains anonymous, and the exact cryptographic mechanism to decipher K4 is still publicly debated.
